02

The Objective

Our goal is to create an Active Electromagnetic System where a magnet hovers by responding to its own position.

  • Stabilize a magnet in mid-air against the force of gravity.
  • Implement a feedback loop to prevent "snapping" or "falling."
  • Utilize Faraday's Law to sense and Ampere's Law to act.
03

Bill of Materials

  • Magnetic Source: 2x Neodymium Disc Magnets
  • The Eye: SS495A Hall Effect Sensor
  • The Muscle: N-Channel MOSFET (IRFZ44N)
  • The Coil: 22-30 AWG Enameled Copper Wire
  • The Core: Iron or Steel Bolt (I-Shape)
  • Power: 12V 2A DC Adapter
04

Winding the Heart

The solenoid is our primary tool for manipulating Maxwell's fields.

  • Wrap roughly 550 turns of copper wire around the iron bolt.
  • Wind "turn-to-turn" in consistent layers to ensure a uniform field.
  • Insulate the bolt with paper or tape first to protect the coating.

05

Faraday's Eye

Without a sensor, Earnshaw's Theorem dictates the magnet will always flip or fall.

  • Mount the Hall Sensor at the base of the electromagnet.
  • The sensor detects the magnetic field strength of the hovering magnet.
  • If the magnet gets too close, the current drops; too far, the current increases.
06

Unifying the Machine

  • Secure the coil to a sturdy vertical frame (cardboard or wood).
  • Connect the coil in series with the MOSFET and your power source.
  • Connect the sensor output to an Arduino Nano or an Op-Amp circuit to control the MOSFET gate.
