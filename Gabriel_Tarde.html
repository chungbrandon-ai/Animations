Gabriel Tarde (1843–1904)

A French sociologist and criminologist who argued that society is not a collective entity, but an aggregate of individuals influencing one another through imitation.

His "Laws of Imitation" (1890) proposed that new ideas spread through three paths: close contact, imitation of superiors, and replacement of old habits. This process creates the classic S-curve seen in modern innovation diffusion theory.
